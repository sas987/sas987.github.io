Soils of India — 6 Major Types

★ Match soil to region — confirmed BPSC pattern
Soil TypeRegionCrops
Alluvial SoilNorthern Plains (Punjab, Haryana, UP, Bihar, WB, Assam); also east & west coastal plains, river deltasWheat, rice, sugarcane, jute, oilseeds (most fertile; covers ~40% of India)
Black Soil (Regur)Deccan Trap region — Maharashtra, MP, Gujarat, parts of AP, Karnataka, TNCotton (also called "black cotton soil"); also wheat, jowar, oilseeds; high moisture retention
Red SoilEastern + Southern Plateau — TN, Karnataka, Telangana, AP, Odisha, parts of Jharkhand, ChhattisgarhRice (in low areas), pulses, oilseeds, millets; red color due to iron oxide
Laterite SoilHigh rainfall hilly areas — Western Ghats, Eastern Ghats, Assam Hills, Rajmahal Hills, Meghalaya, parts of OdishaTea, coffee, rubber, cashew; formed by intense leaching; poor in fertility (porous)
Mountain (Forest) SoilHimalayas, hill regions (HP, J&K, Sikkim, Arunachal, Darjeeling, Nilgiri, Western Ghats)Tea, coffee, spices, fruits, medicinal plants
Arid / Desert SoilRajasthan (Thar), parts of Gujarat (Kutch), Haryana, Punjab (south)Bajra, jowar (drought-resistant); saline patches; high salt content
Alluvial Soil — Two Sub-Types (Critical for BPSC)
Sub-TypeDescriptionBihar Connection
Khadar (Newer Alluvium)Newer, deposited annually by floods; most fertile; clay/silt-richBihar's annual flood deposits along Ganga, Kosi → most fertile farmland
Bhangar (Older Alluvium)Older, higher terraces; contains kankar (calcareous concretions); less fertileBhangar uplands in Bihar between rivers
"Black cotton soil (Regur)" = formed from Deccan Trap basalt; found in Maharashtra-MP-Gujarat. Best for cotton. Frequently asked in BPSC. "Khadar vs Bhangar" — Khadar is newer + more fertile.
★ Soil Coverage in India (by Area)
  • Alluvial: ~40% (largest coverage) — Indo-Gangetic plain
  • Black: ~15% — Deccan Trap
  • Red: ~18% — Peninsular plateau
  • Laterite: Smaller pockets — high rainfall areas
  • Mountain: ~5% — hill regions
  • Arid: ~5% — NW India
PYQ Pattern — BPSC
"Regur soil is found mainly in which region of India?"
Deccan Trap region — Maharashtra, MP, Gujarat (black cotton soil; from basalt weathering)
PYQ Pattern — BPSC
"Laterite soil is formed by which process?"
Intense leaching in tropical hot-wet conditions — found in Western Ghats, Eastern Ghats, Meghalaya

★ Key Facts on Indian Forests
  • Most common forest type: Tropical Deciduous (Monsoon forest)
  • Most diverse: Tropical Evergreen (Western Ghats & NE)
  • Total forest cover (2021): ~21.71% of India's geographical area
  • State with most forest area (km²): Madhya Pradesh
  • State with highest % forest cover: Mizoram (~85%)

Indian Cropping Seasons — Kharif, Rabi, Zaid

★ Confirmed BPSC frequency — match crop to season
SeasonMonthsMajor Crops
KharifSown June-July (with SW monsoon); harvested Sept-OctRice, Maize, Jowar, Bajra, Cotton, Sugarcane, Soybean, Groundnut, Jute, Tobacco
RabiSown Oct-Nov (after monsoon); harvested March-AprilWheat, Barley, Gram, Mustard, Peas, Linseed; relies on Western Disturbances rain
ZaidSown March-April; harvested June-July (between Rabi and Kharif)Watermelon, Cucumber, Muskmelon, Vegetables, Fodder crops; irrigation-dependent
Mnemonic:
Kharif ("monsoon crops"): grown WITH the monsoon — needs hot wet weather.
Rabi ("winter crops"): grown in COLD winter, harvested in spring — needs cool weather.
Zaid ("summer crops"): between the two main seasons — needs irrigation, can't depend on monsoon.
"Which crop is sown in Kharif season?" → Rice, Maize, Cotton, Sugarcane. "Wheat is a ___ crop?" → Rabi crop. "Cash crops grown in summer?" → Zaid (watermelon, cucumber). Match-the-pair format common.

TypeDescription

Commercial farmingFor market; large scale; mechanised (e.g., Punjab, Haryana)
Plantation farmingSingle cash crop on large area (tea, coffee, rubber, sugarcane)
Intensive farmingHigh inputs (fertilisers, water) per unit area — Indo-Gangetic plain
Extensive farmingLow inputs over large area — Rajasthan dryland

Major Food Crops of India

★ Match crop to leading producer state — high BPSC frequency
Rice (Paddy) KHARIF · STAPLE #1 India is 2nd largest producer (after China)
Climate: Hot (20-27°C) and wet (100+ cm rainfall); flooded paddy fields.
Soil: Clayey loam, alluvial.
Leading producer: West Bengal (#1); UP, Punjab, Bihar, AP, Odisha also major.
Cropping pattern: Mainly Kharif; but Punjab/Haryana grow it as commercial crop with heavy irrigation; 3 crops a year in West Bengal (Aus, Aman, Boro).
India's #1 production: West Bengal.
India's #1 yield (productivity): Punjab.
India is world #2 producer, also #2 exporter (after Thailand).
Wheat RABI · STAPLE #2 India is 2nd largest producer (after China)
Climate: Cool growing season (10-15°C) + warm ripening (~21°C); 50-75 cm rainfall.
Soil: Well-drained loamy/alluvial; black soil also suitable.
Leading producer: Uttar Pradesh (#1); Punjab, Haryana, MP, Bihar also major.
Green Revolution belt: Punjab, Haryana, Western UP.
India produces ~14% of world's wheat.
Maize (Corn) KHARIF + RABI + ZAID Versatile — grown in all 3 seasons
Climate: Hot (~21-27°C); 50-100 cm rainfall.
Leading producer: Karnataka (#1); Madhya Pradesh, Bihar, Tamil Nadu also major.
Bihar position: 3rd largest producer in India; Khagaria district is "Maize Capital".
Uses: Food, fodder, ethanol, starch.
Millets (Coarse Cereals) KHARIF · DRY-LAND "Nutri-Cereals" · 2023 Int'l Year of Millets
Major millets: Jowar (Sorghum), Bajra (Pearl Millet), Ragi (Finger Millet).
Climate: Drought-resistant; suitable for poor soils.
Leading producers:
  • • Jowar: Maharashtra (#1) — Karnataka also major
  • • Bajra: Rajasthan (#1) — UP, Haryana, Gujarat also major
  • • Ragi: Karnataka (#1) — TN also major
2023 = International Year of Millets (proposed by India; UN declared).
Pulses KHARIF + RABI India is world #1 producer AND consumer
Major pulses: Tur (Arhar), Gram (Chana), Urad, Moong, Masoor (Lentil).
Climate: Dry, hot; nitrogen-fixing (improves soil fertility).
Leading producer: Madhya Pradesh (#1 for pulses overall); Rajasthan, Maharashtra, UP, Karnataka.
Masoor (Lentil): Bihar is #1.
Tur (Arhar): Maharashtra is #1.
India produces ~25% of world's pulses.
★ Quick State-Leader Mapping (Major Food Crops)

  • Wheat: UP #1; Punjab #2
  • Maize: Karnataka #1; Bihar #3
  • Jowar: Maharashtra #1
  • Bajra: Rajasthan #1
  • Ragi: Karnataka #1
  • Pulses: Madhya Pradesh #1

★ India's "Mineral Storehouse" — Chota Nagpur Plateau

The Chota Nagpur Plateau (Jharkhand mainly + parts of Odisha, WB, Bihar, Chhattisgarh) is India's mineral storehouse. Contains:

  • Coal (Jharia, Bokaro), Iron Ore (Singhbhum), Copper (Singhbhum), Bauxite, Mica, Uranium, Manganese, Limestone
  • Reason: Pre-Cambrian rocks formed under tectonic conditions favorable for ore concentration
  • "Ruhr of India": Damodar Valley (coal belt)
"India's only diamond mine is at?" → Panna, Madhya Pradesh. "India's main uranium mine?" → Jaduguda, Jharkhand. "Kolar Gold Fields?" → Karnataka. "India's coal #1 producer?" → Jharkhand. Frequently asked in BPSC.

Section 15

BPSC Trap Patterns — Indian Geography

15 common traps that catch even prepared candidates
⚠ TRAP 1 — Kangchenjunga vs Nanda Devi

"Highest peak in India" = Kangchenjunga (8,586m) — but on India-Nepal border. "Highest peak entirely in India" = Nanda Devi (7,816m). Read question precisely.

⚠ TRAP 2 — K2 is NOT in Himalayas

K2 (Godwin Austen) is in Karakoram Range, NOT Himalayas. Karakoram is part of Trans-Himalayan system.

⚠ TRAP 3 — Mahananda Origin

Mahananda originates in Darjeeling Hills (West Bengal), NOT Nepal. The only major North Bihar river NOT from Nepal.

⚠ TRAP 4 — Luni's End

Luni ends in Rann of Kutch, NOT Gulf of Khambhat. Lost in marshy land.

⚠ TRAP 5 — Indravati-Sabari Tributaries

Indravati and Sabari are tributaries of Godavari (BPSC CDPO 2018 confirmed), NOT Krishna or Mahanadi.

⚠ TRAP 6 — Narmada-Tapi Don't Form Deltas

They form estuaries (rift valleys with steep gradient). Only east-flowing peninsular rivers form deltas.

⚠ TRAP 7 — Coromandel Rainfall Months

Coromandel coast gets October-November rainfall from NE monsoon (52nd BPSC confirmed), NOT SW monsoon.

⚠ TRAP 8 — Mango Shower Region

Mango Shower is in Karnataka coffee belt (43rd BPSC), NOT in Bihar/Bengal. Don't confuse with Kalbaisakhi (Bengal NE region).

⚠ TRAP 9 — Amritsar-Shimla Difference

Same latitude — different climate due to altitude (53rd-55th BPSC), NOT distance from sea.

⚠ TRAP 10 — India's First National Park

Jim Corbett (1936) is India's FIRST National Park. Don't confuse with first Tiger Reserve (Corbett, 1973).

⚠ TRAP 11 — UP vs Maharashtra Sugar

UP is #1 in cane production; Maharashtra is #1 in sugar production (efficiency difference).

⚠ TRAP 12 — West Bengal vs Bihar Jute

West Bengal is #1 in jute; Bihar is #2. Don't confuse with rice (also WB #1 production, Punjab #1 yield).

⚠ TRAP 13 — TISCO First Plant Year

TISCO Jamshedpur established in 1907, NOT 1903 (which is sugar - Khopoli) or 1853 (railways).

⚠ TRAP 14 — Saar vs Ruhr Confusion

India equivalent: Damodar Valley = "Ruhr of India" (coal belt). Don't confuse with mineral belts in Bihar/Jharkhand.

⚠ TRAP 15 — Manchester of India

Modern "Manchester of India" = Ahmedabad. Historic was Mumbai (first cotton mill 1854). "Manchester of South India" = Coimbatore. "Manchester of the East" = Kanpur (also "Leather City").
