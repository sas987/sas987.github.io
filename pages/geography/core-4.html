Section 01

Cultural-Geographical Regions of Bihar

5 ancient regions still defining Bihar today — extremely high frequency in BPSC because they connect history + geography + culture in one question

"Bihar's modern districts are administrative; its identity is divided by ancient cultural regions — Mithila, Magadh, Bhojpur, Anga, and Vajji. Every Bihari knows their region. Every BPSC paper tests this knowledge.

Mithila North Bihar · Sita's land · Maithili language
Capital (ancient): Mithilanagari (associated with Janakpur, now in Nepal). Modern districts in Mithila: Darbhanga, Madhubani, Sitamarhi, Sheohar, Samastipur, Begusarai (partly), Khagaria (partly), Saharsa, Madhepura, Supaul, parts of Muzaffarpur and Vaishali.

Cultural identity: Maithili language (Sanskritic; one of India's 22 Scheduled Languages). Madhubani Painting (GI 2007). Mithila Makhana (GI 2022). Sikki craft.

Geography: Flood-prone Himalayan rivers belt; chaur wetlands; Kosi-Bagmati-Kamla flow through.
Famous figureKing Janaka, Sita
LanguageMaithili (Scheduled)
GI productsMadhubani Painting, Mithila Makhana
FestivalsChhath (Mithila origin), Madhushravani
Magadh South-Central Bihar · ancient empire heartland · Magahi language
Capital (ancient): Rajgir (initially), then Pataliputra (modern Patna). Heart of Maurya, Gupta, Pala dynasties. Modern districts: Patna (partly), Nalanda, Gaya, Aurangabad, Nawada, Jehanabad, Arwal, parts of Bhojpur.

Cultural identity: Magahi language. Buddhism's birthplace (Bodh Gaya, Rajgir, Nalanda). Hindu pitra-moksha (Vishnupad, Gaya). Magahi Paan (GI 2017). Silao Khaja (GI 2018).

Geography: Drier south Bihar; less flood-prone; rocky terrain (Barabar Hills, Rajgir Hills); seasonal Phalgu river.

Cultural identity: Bhojpuri language (one of India's most-spoken languages — over 50 million speakers globally including diaspora in Mauritius, Suriname, Fiji). Folk traditions: Holi songs, Birha, Sohar.

Why Bihar has Hot Springs

Bihar's hot springs are concentrated in Munger and Rajgir hill regions — areas of Vindhyan-era rock formations with deep fissures. The Munger-Rajgir thermal belt is part of the broader peninsular India hot-spring system. Geothermal water rises through tectonic fractures from depths of 1–3 km.

★ Why So Many Ashokan Sites in Bihar?

Bihar (Magadha) was the capital of the Mauryan Empire. Pataliputra was Ashoka's seat of power. Champaran was on the route Buddha took to Lumbini (Nepal) — Ashoka erected pillars marking Buddhist pilgrimage sites along this route. Bihar has the highest concentration of Ashokan pillars in India.

Environmental Issues of Bihar

Arsenic, fluoride, pollution — increasingly important in BPSC current-affairs-style questions
⚠ Arsenic Contamination — Most Asked Issue

The Arsenic Belt of Bihar: Found in groundwater of districts along the Ganga floodplain — Patna, Bhojpur, Vaishali, Saran, Buxar, Begusarai, Khagaria, Bhagalpur, Munger, Samastipur, Lakhisarai.

13 districts officially declared arsenic-affected. WHO permissible limit: 10 ppb. Bihar areas show 50–500+ ppb in some samples. Causes: chronic arsenicosis, skin lesions, cancer.

Source: Geogenic — natural arsenic from sediments of Himalayan rivers being released into groundwater under specific aquifer conditions.

⚠ Fluoride Contamination

Fluoride-Affected Districts: Mainly in South Bihar — Banka, Aurangabad, Gaya, Nawada, Jamui, Munger, Sheikhpura, Kaimur, Rohtas. WHO limit: 1.5 ppm. Bihar areas show 2–5 ppm.

Effects: Dental fluorosis, skeletal fluorosis (joint pain, bone deformity).

Pattern: Geogenic source — fluoride leaches from granite/laterite rocks of Chota Nagpur Plateau fringe.

Climate Change Impact on Bihar
  • Increasing rainfall variability: heavier rain in fewer days → both more floods AND more droughts in same year
  • Earlier monsoon retreat: Reducing growing season for kharif crops
  • Heat wave intensification: Gaya temperatures crossing 48°C more frequently
  • Glacial-fed river volatility: Kosi flow patterns becoming more erratic due to Himalayan glacial melt
  • Bihar's State Action Plan on Climate Change (SAPCC) — covers agriculture, water, forestry adaptation
Section 16

Geological Foundation of Bihar

Rock formations and tectonic setting — explains why Bihar floods, why South Bihar has minerals, why hot springs exist
📜 The 3 Geological Zones of Bihar
  • Zone 1 — Northern Alluvial Plain: Newer alluvium from Himalayan rivers; soft sediments; depth up to 4,000m. Earthquake-prone (Zone V).
  • Zone 2 — Ganga Alluvial Belt: Older alluvium; the Ganga dividing line; mixed sediments.
  • Zone 3 — Southern Plateau Fringe: Hard rocks of Vindhyan + Chota Nagpur extension; Pre-Cambrian rocks; mineral-bearing; older formations.
Rock FormationAgeBihar Region
Vindhyan SystemProterozoic (1500-700 mya)Kaimur Plateau — limestone, sandstone, shale
GondwanaPermo-Triassic (300-200 mya)Small patches in southern Bihar fringe (mostly went to Jharkhand)
Quaternary AlluviumPleistocene-Holocene (2.5 mya - present)Most of Bihar plains — both Bhangar (older) and Khadar (newer)
Chota Nagpur Granite GneissArchean-Proterozoic (3-1.5 bya)Banka, Jamui, Munger fringe — laterite weathered surface
Tectonic Setting
  • Main Boundary Thrust (MBT) — runs along Bihar-Nepal border
  • Main Frontal Thrust (MFT) — slightly south of MBT
  • These thrusts make North Bihar earthquake-prone (Zone V)
  • The 1934 earthquake was caused by movement along MBT
  • Bihar's hot springs (Rajgir, Munger) are in the Vindhyan rock zone — fissure-fed geothermal water

Section 18

BPSC Trap Patterns — Specific to Bihar Geography

Common traps that catch even prepared candidates — read these before exam
⚠ TRAP 1 — Old vs New District Names
"Shahabad district is famous for which industry?"
Trick: Shahabad no longer exists. It was split in 1972 into Bhojpur, Buxar, Rohtas, Kaimur. Always check if the question references a district that exists today vs historically.
⚠ TRAP 2 — National Park vs Tiger Reserve vs WLS
"How many National Parks does Bihar have?"
Answer: 1 (one) — Valmiki National Park (which is also a Tiger Reserve). Don't confuse with Wildlife Sanctuaries (multiple). Bihar's only National Park = Valmiki.
⚠ TRAP 3 — Karmnasa Direction
"Which Bihar river flows in opposite direction to most others?"
Answer: Karmnasa — flows westward (toward Ganga via UP), opposite to virtually all Bihar rivers which flow east or south. Frequently asked in tricky form.
⚠ TRAP 4 — Burhi Gandak Origin
"Which North Bihar river originates within India, not Nepal?"
Answer: Burhi Gandak — originates from Chautarwa Chaur in Sheohar district, Bihar. Most candidates assume all N. Bihar rivers come from Nepal — this is the exception.
⚠ TRAP 5 — Mahananda Origin
"Mahananda originates in Nepal — true or false?"
Answer: FALSE. Mahananda originates in Darjeeling Hills, West Bengal (not Nepal). It enters Bihar at Kishanganj. The only major North Bihar river not from Nepal.
⚠ TRAP 6 — Bihar State Animal Confusion
"What is Bihar's State Animal?"
Answer: Gaur (Indian Bison). Don't confuse with Gangetic Dolphin which is India's National Aquatic Animal (found in Vikramshila Sanctuary). State animal ≠ national aquatic animal.
⚠ TRAP 7 — Jardalu vs Other Mangos
"Bihar's GI-tagged mango is from which district?"
Answer: Jardalu Mango — Bhagalpur. Don't confuse with Digha Malda (Patna) or Langra (originated UP). Only Jardalu has GI status (2018).
⚠ TRAP 8 — Sonepur Mela Location
"Sonepur Mela is held in which district?"
Answer: Vaishali district (at Sonepur town in Hajipur). Many candidates write "Saran" because Sonepur is across Gandak from Saran — but officially it's in Vaishali district administratively.
⚠ TRAP 9 — Sone vs Son spelling
"Sone Canal" or "Son Canal"?
Both spellings appear. Official Bihar government documents often use "Sone Canal System" for the canal network, while the river itself is often spelled "Son" or "Sone". BPSC accepts both. Don't lose marks over spelling.
⚠ TRAP 10 — Bodh Gaya District
"In which district is Bodh Gaya located?"
Answer: Gaya district — Bodh Gaya is a town within Gaya district (~13 km south). Don't write "Bodhgaya" as a separate district — it isn't one.
⚠ TRAP 11 — Tropic of Cancer Districts
"Tropic of Cancer passes through which Bihar districts?"
Answer: Gaya and Bhagalpur primarily (also touches Jamui, Banka). Don't write "all of South Bihar" — specifically these two districts in the question.
⚠ TRAP 12 — Mithila Makhana vs Bihar Makhana
"Mithila Makhana got GI in 2022 — but wasn't it always called Bihar Makhana?"
Answer: It was renamed from "Bihar Makhana" to "Mithila Makhana" when GI tag was approved in 2022 — to specifically credit the Mithila region.
⚠ TRAP 13 — IARI Origin
"Where was IARI (Indian Agricultural Research Institute) originally established?"
Answer: Pusa, Samastipur (Bihar) in 1903. Later shifted to Delhi (1936) after a major earthquake. The name "Pusa" survived as "Pusa Institute" in Delhi.
⚠ TRAP 14 — Champaran Satyagraha vs Indigo
"Champaran Satyagraha was about which crop?"
Answer: Indigo (Neel) — not sugarcane. The Tinkathia system forced farmers to grow indigo on 3/20 of their land. Often confused with sugarcane.
⚠ TRAP 15 — Bihar's Number of Bordering States
"Bihar borders how many other Indian states?"
Answer: 3 states — Uttar Pradesh, Jharkhand, West Bengal. Plus 1 country: Nepal. Don't include Nepal as a "state".
