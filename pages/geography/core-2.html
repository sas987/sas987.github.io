Land Use Pattern of Bihar
  • Net Sown Area: ~58–60% of Bihar's total land area (one of the highest in India)
  • Gross Cropped Area: ~80–85 lakh hectares
  • Cropping Intensity: ~144% (high — meaning multi-cropping is common)
  • Forest cover: ~7.7% (way below national average of 24%)
  • Net Irrigated Area: ~63% of net sown area; main sources: tube wells (~60%), canals (~30%), other
  • Average landholding size: ~0.39 hectare — Bihar has the smallest average landholding in India (mostly marginal farmers)
Section 02

Major Kharif Crops

Sown in June–July, harvested in October–November. Monsoon-dependent crops.
🌾

Rice (Paddy) — Main Kharif Crop

Bihar is among the top 4 rice producing states in India. Rice covers the largest area of all crops in Bihar. The state has the geographical advantage of fertile alluvial soils, abundant water (Ganga + tributaries), and high humidity — ideal for paddy cultivation.

Major districts: Rohtas (called "Rice Bowl of Bihar"), Kaimur, Buxar, Bhojpur, West Champaran, Aurangabad, Patna, Madhubani.

RICE BOWL: ROHTAS GI VARIETY: KATARNI (BHAGALPUR) GI VARIETY: MARCHA (W. CHAMPARAN)
🌽

Maize — Bihar's Strategic Crop

Bihar is the 3rd largest maize producer in India (after Karnataka and Madhya Pradesh in some years). Maize is grown in all three seasons (Kharif, Rabi, Zaid) — a unique feature.

The Kosi region (Khagaria, Saharsa, Madhepura, Begusarai, Samastipur, Purnia) is the maize hub. Khagaria is the largest maize-producing district in Bihar.

MAIZE BOWL: KHAGARIA 3-SEASON CROP BIHAR RANK: 3rd IN INDIA
🟫

Jute — Bihar's Cash Crop

Bihar is the 2nd largest jute producer in India (after West Bengal). Jute thrives in humid clayey soil with high water availability — perfect match for the Kosi-Mahananda belt.

Major districts: Purnia, Katihar, Araria, Kishanganj, Saharsa. Together they form India's "second jute belt" after West Bengal.

JUTE HUB: PURNIA-KATIHAR BIHAR RANK: 2nd IN INDIA
🍬

Sugarcane — Champaran's Crop

Sugarcane was historically Bihar's most important commercial crop. The famous Champaran Satyagraha (1917) by Gandhi was related to sugarcane indigo cultivation issues.

Major sugarcane districts: West Champaran, East Champaran, Gopalganj, Siwan, Saran, Muzaffarpur. The state has multiple sugar mills clustered here.

SUGAR BOWL: CHAMPARAN HISTORIC: 1917 SATYAGRAHA
🟤

Pulses (Kharif) — Arhar, Urad, Mung

Bihar is among the top pulse-producing states. Kharif pulses include Arhar (Tur), Urad, Mung. Bihar is the largest producer of Masoor (Lentil) in India — though Masoor is a Rabi pulse.

Districts: Patna, Aurangabad, Gaya, Nalanda, Nawada (south Bihar dominates pulse cultivation due to less waterlogging).

MASOOR: BIHAR #1 IN INDIA SOUTH BIHAR DOMINANT
🌿

Tobacco

Bihar produces a significant share of India's tobacco. Vaishali and Samastipur are the leading tobacco districts. Bihar's tobacco is mainly used for chewing (gutka, khaini) and bidi-making.

TOBACCO HUB: VAISHALI, SAMASTIPUR
Section 03

Major Rabi Crops

Sown in October–November, harvested in March–April. Winter crops.
🌾

Wheat — Main Rabi Crop

Bihar is India's 5th largest wheat producer. Wheat has expanded rapidly in Bihar after the Green Revolution. It is grown across all districts but dominantly in the Ganga plains.

Major wheat districts: Rohtas, Buxar, Bhojpur, Kaimur, Aurangabad, Patna (south Bihar dominates wheat too).

WHEAT BOWL: ROHTAS-BUXAR-BHOJPUR BIHAR RANK: 5th IN INDIA
🥜

Pulses (Rabi) — Gram & Lentil (Masoor)

Bihar is India's #1 producer of Masoor (Lentil). This is one of the most asked agricultural facts in BPSC. Other Rabi pulses include Gram (Chana), Pea, Khesari.

Districts: Patna, Aurangabad, Gaya, Bhojpur, Buxar — south Bihar dominates due to drier conditions ideal for pulses.

★ MASOOR: BIHAR #1 IN INDIA PRIMARY ZONE: SOUTH BIHAR
🌻

Oilseeds — Mustard, Linseed, Rapeseed

Mustard (Sarson) is the dominant oilseed in Bihar. Linseed (Tisi) is grown in flood-prone areas as a recovery crop. Districts: Madhubani, Darbhanga, Patna, Aurangabad.

DOMINANT: MUSTARD RECOVERY CROP: LINSEED
🌾

Barley

Grown in moisture-deficient areas. Districts: Aurangabad, Gaya, Nawada — drier south Bihar zones with Bhangar soil.

Section 04

Horticulture — Bihar's Pride

Bihar leads India in multiple horticultural products — every district-fruit pair is testable
🍒

Litchi — Muzaffarpur is "Litchi Capital of India"

Bihar produces approximately ~75% of India's total litchi. The "Shahi Litchi" of Muzaffarpur received GI tag in 2018. The litchi belt covers Muzaffarpur, East Champaran, Vaishali, Samastipur, and Begusarai.

India is the 2nd largest litchi producer in the world (after China) — Bihar dominates within India.

★ GI: SHAHI LITCHI 2018 LITCHI CAPITAL: MUZAFFARPUR BIHAR ~75% OF INDIA'S LITCHI
🥥

Makhana (Fox Nut / Lotus Seed) — World's Largest Producer

Bihar produces approximately 90% of the world's makhana. Mithila Makhana received GI tag in 2022. The makhana belt is concentrated in Darbhanga, Madhubani, Sitamarhi, Saharsa, Supaul, Katihar, Purnia, Madhepura — wherever chaur wetlands exist.

Darbhanga is the "Makhana Capital of India". The Makhana Research Institute is located in Darbhanga.

★ GI: MITHILA MAKHANA 2022 MAKHANA CAPITAL: DARBHANGA BIHAR ~90% OF WORLD MAKHANA
🥭

Mango — Multiple Famous Varieties

Bihar is among the top mango-producing states. Famous varieties:

• Jardalu Mango (Bhagalpur) — got GI tag in 2018; aromatic, fibreless
• Digha Malda — from Digha (Patna) area; sent to PM during Independence Day
• Bombai/Bombay — Vaishali area
• Langra — also grown in Bihar (originated in Varanasi)

★ GI: JARDALU MANGO 2018 MANGO HUB: BHAGALPUR, DIGHA
🍌

Banana — Hajipur is the Banana Capital

Bihar is among India's top banana producers. Hajipur (Vaishali district) is the most famous banana center — its Chinia banana variety is renowned. Other banana districts: Vaishali, Samastipur, Bhagalpur, Khagaria.

BANANA HUB: HAJIPUR (VAISHALI) VARIETY: CHINIA
🍃

Magahi Paan — Magadh's GI Treasure

Magahi Paan received GI tag in 2017. Cultivated in the Magadh region: Aurangabad, Gaya, Nawada, Nalanda. Known for its distinctive sweet aroma and softness.

★ GI: MAGAHI PAAN 2017 REGION: MAGADH
🥬

Vegetables — Bihar is India's #3 Producer

Bihar is the 3rd largest vegetable producer in India. The Patna-Vaishali-Samastipur corridor (along Ganga) is the vegetable belt. Major vegetables: Potato, Onion, Cauliflower, Brinjal, Okra, Cucumber, Tomato.

BIHAR RANK: #3 IN VEGETABLES BELT: PATNA-VAISHALI-SAMASTIPUR
Section 05

Geographical Indication (GI) Tags of Bihar

★ Highest-frequency BPSC topic — every GI tag's name + district + year is testable

A Geographical Indication (GI) is a sign on products with a specific geographical origin and qualities/reputation due to that origin. Bihar holds 9 GI tags currently. Memorise all by name, region, and year.

🥥
Mithila Makhana
GI Tag: 2022 · Districts: Darbhanga, Madhubani, Sitamarhi, Saharsa, Supaul, Katihar, Purnia, Madhepura
Lotus seed cultivated in chaur wetlands of Mithila region. Bihar produces ~90% of world's makhana. Renamed from "Bihar Makhana" to "Mithila Makhana" specifically to credit the Mithila region. Status: Superfood, exported globally.
🍒
Shahi Litchi
GI Tag: 2018 · District: Muzaffarpur (& adjoining East Champaran, Vaishali, Samastipur)
Premium litchi variety with distinctive deep pink-red color, aromatic flavor, and high sugar content. Muzaffarpur is the "Litchi Capital of India". Bihar's litchi production is ~75% of India's total.
🥭
Jardalu Mango
GI Tag: 2018 · District: Bhagalpur
Aromatic, fibreless mango variety. Distinctive elongated shape and slight saffron-yellow color. PM Modi has gifted this to foreign dignitaries. Mainly grown in Bhagalpur and adjoining Banka districts.
🌾
Katarni Rice (Chawal)
GI Tag: 2018 · District: Bhagalpur (also Banka, Munger)
Aromatic premium rice variety. Distinctive scented grains; once exported to Europe in colonial times. Grown in Bhagalpur — same region as Jardalu Mango. Smaller grains, fragrant when cooked.
🍃
Magahi Paan (Betel Leaf)
GI Tag: 2017 · Districts: Aurangabad, Gaya, Nawada, Nalanda (Magadh region)
Distinctive soft, sweet betel leaf cultivated in the Magadh region (south-central Bihar). Used in pan and religious ceremonies. The leaves have a unique aroma and texture due to soil + climate of the region.
🎨
Madhubani Painting (Mithila Painting)
GI Tag: 2007 · District: Madhubani (and Mithila region — Darbhanga, Sitamarhi)
Traditional folk art originating from Mithila region. Practiced by women on walls, then on cloth and paper. Themes: Hindu mythology, nature, social events. Internationally famous — has put Madhubani district on the global map.
🧶
Bhagalpuri Silk (Tussar Silk)
GI Tag: 2013 · District: Bhagalpur
Bhagalpur is called the "Silk City of Bihar". Produces high-quality Tussar silk. Famous worldwide for fine sarees and fabrics. Industry has run for over 200 years.
🍯
Silao Khaja
GI Tag: 2018 · District: Nalanda (Silao town)
A traditional layered, crispy sweet pastry. Made in Silao town between Rajgir and Nalanda. Documented in Buddhist texts — Buddha and his disciples are said to have consumed it. Layers of paper-thin maida fried with ghee and dipped in syrup.
🌶️
Marcha Rice (Mirchaiya)
GI Tag: 2023 · District: West Champaran
Aromatic rice variety with grains shaped like green chilli (marcha). Cultivated in West Champaran. Distinctive fragrance, fluffy when cooked. Newer GI addition (2023) — likely to appear in 71st BPSC onwards.

Section 11

Major Irrigation Projects

3–4 questions per paper — match river to barrage to district
Sone Canal System on Son River · Indrapuri Barrage at Dehri-on-Son OLDEST IN BIHAR
Bihar's oldest and largest canal irrigation system. Originally built by British in 1873–74 (one of India's oldest irrigation systems). The current Indrapuri Barrage (1968) replaced the older anicut.

Components: Eastern Sone Canal + Western Sone Canal → irrigates Rohtas, Bhojpur, Aurangabad, Patna, Arwal districts.
Modernization: Currently undergoing extensive modernization to improve efficiency.
Gandak Project on Gandak River · Valmiki Nagar Barrage INDO-NEPAL
Joint Indo-Nepal project — Valmiki Nagar Barrage on Gandak river in West Champaran. Inaugurated 1959.

Components: Eastern Main Canal (irrigates Bihar) + Western Main Canal (irrigates UP and Nepal).
Beneficiary districts: West Champaran, East Champaran, Gopalganj, Siwan, Saran, Muzaffarpur, Vaishali, Samastipur, Begusarai.
Also called: Gandak Barrage / Bishambharpur Barrage.
Kosi Project on Kosi River · Bhimnagar Barrage at Supaul INDO-NEPAL · 1959
Indo-Nepal joint project (1959) — Bhimnagar Barrage on Kosi at Bihar-Nepal border, Supaul district.

Components: Eastern Kosi Main Canal (Bihar) + Western Kosi Main Canal (Nepal & Bihar).
Beneficiary districts: Saharsa, Madhepura, Supaul, Khagaria, Katihar, Purnia.
Critical role: Flood control + irrigation. Has been controversial due to silt accumulation.
Bagmati Project on Bagmati River · Multi-purpose FLOOD CONTROL
Major flood control + irrigation project in Sitamarhi, Muzaffarpur, Darbhanga belt. Embankments built along Bagmati to control floods + irrigation channels.
Triveni Canal Project on Gandak (Triveni junction) CHAMPARAN
Older project (pre-Independence era). Diverts Gandak waters for West Champaran agriculture. Triveni junction is where Gandak enters Bihar from Nepal.
Kamla Project (Kamla Barrage at Jainagar) on Kamla-Balan River · Jainagar, Madhubani MITHILA REGION
Barrage at Jainagar (Madhubani) on Kamla river. Provides irrigation to Madhubani, Darbhanga, Saharsa.
Eastern & Western Kosi Main Canals distributaries of Kosi Project SUB-PROJECTS
Eastern Kosi Main Canal (EKMC) — runs through Saharsa, Madhepura, Khagaria, Purnia.
Western Kosi Main Canal (WKMC) — irrigates Supaul, parts of Madhubani, partly Nepal.
★ Sources of Irrigation in Bihar (Memorise %)
  • Tube wells / wells: ~60% (largest source — groundwater dominant)
  • Canals: ~30% (Sone canal, Gandak canal, Kosi canal etc.)
  • Tanks & ponds: ~5%
  • Other sources: ~5%
  • Net Irrigated Area: ~63% of net sown area

  • NH-31 — runs along North Bihar (Hajipur–Begusarai–Khagaria–Purnia)
  • NH-77 / NH-22 — connects Patna–Sitamarhi to Nepal
  • NH-57 (now NH-27) — East-West Corridor through North Bihar
  • National Waterway-1 (NW-1) — Ganga from Allahabad to Haldia; passes through Bihar (Patna, Munger, Bhagalpur); India's first national waterway; 1,620 km total

Khagaria — fertile Kosi alluvial soil + abundant water + suitable climate; Bihar is India's 3rd largest maize producer

Begusarai district; 2nd oldest oil refinery in India (commissioned 1964; after Digboi Assam)

Masoor (Lentil) — Bihar is India's #1 producer; mainly grown in south Bihar

Son river at Dehri-on-Son, Rohtas (1968); irrigates Rohtas, Bhojpur, Aurangabad, Patna, Arwal via Eastern & Western Sone Canals

Hajipur (Vaishali district)
