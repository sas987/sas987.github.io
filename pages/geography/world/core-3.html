★ Part 3 — The Final Lock

Part 3 covers the most "memorisable but high-yield" world geography content in BPSC: country capitals, currencies, parliament names, border lines (Radcliffe, McMahon, etc.), recent name changes, and international organizations. These are pure factual recall questions — no analytical depth needed. Memorise the tables and you're done.

"By end of Part 3, no World Geography question can surprise you. You've covered: physical earth (Part 1), atmosphere/oceans/landforms (Part 2), and now country-level human geography (Part 3). Combined: ~10 marks of world geography locked at near-100% accuracy."

Section 11

Regional Groupings & Alliances

Match acronym to members + HQ — high BPSC frequency
SAARCSOUTH ASIA
South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation. Founded: 1985 in Dhaka. HQ: Kathmandu, Nepal.
8 members: India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Nepal, Bhutan, Maldives, Afghanistan (joined 2007).
Observers: 9 countries including USA, China, Japan, Iran.
ASEANSE ASIA
Association of Southeast Asian Nations. Founded: 1967 in Bangkok. HQ: Jakarta, Indonesia.
10 members: Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines, Singapore, Thailand (founders) + Brunei, Vietnam, Laos, Myanmar, Cambodia.
Note: Timor-Leste joining in 2025-26.
European Union (EU)EUROPE
Founded: 1993 (Maastricht Treaty); evolved from EEC (1957). HQ: Brussels, Belgium.
27 member states (after UK's Brexit in 2020).
Eurozone: 20 EU countries using Euro. Schengen Area: 27 countries with free movement (most EU + few non-EU like Switzerland, Iceland, Norway).
NATOMILITARY
North Atlantic Treaty Organization. Founded: 1949. HQ: Brussels, Belgium.
32 members (latest: Sweden in 2024). USA, UK, France, Germany, Canada are key.
Article 5: "Attack on one is attack on all" — collective defense.
BRICSEMERGING ECONOMIES
Founded: 2009 (Yekaterinburg) as BRIC; S. Africa joined 2010. No permanent HQ (rotating); New Development Bank HQ: Shanghai.
5 founding members: Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa.
Expanded BRICS+ (2024): Added Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, UAE.
G7RICH NATIONS
7 most advanced economies: USA, UK, France, Germany, Italy, Canada, Japan + EU as observer.
Founded: 1975. No permanent HQ; rotating presidency.
Was G8 (with Russia) until Russia expelled in 2014 over Crimea.
G20MAJOR ECONOMIES
19 countries + EU + AU: G7 + Brazil, Russia, India, China, South Africa (BRICS) + Argentina, Australia, Indonesia, Mexico, Saudi Arabia, South Korea, Turkey + EU + AU (2023).
Founded: 1999. India hosted in 2023; theme: "Vasudhaiva Kutumbakam".
OPECOIL CARTEL
Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries. Founded: 1960 in Baghdad. HQ: Vienna, Austria (since 1965).
13 members: Saudi Arabia, Iran, Iraq, Kuwait, UAE, Venezuela, Nigeria, Algeria, Libya, Angola, Republic of Congo, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon.
OPEC+: OPEC + 10 non-OPEC oil producers (incl. Russia, Mexico).
Commonwealth of NationsFORMER BRITISH
56 member states (mostly former British colonies + a few others like Mozambique, Rwanda, Gabon, Togo).
Founded: 1949 (London Declaration). HQ: London, UK. Head: King Charles III.
India is a member; Pakistan rejoined 2004 (after 1972 exit).
African Union (AU)AFRICA
Successor of OAU (1963). Founded: 2002. HQ: Addis Ababa, Ethiopia.
55 member states (all African countries).
OICISLAMIC
Organisation of Islamic Cooperation. Founded: 1969. HQ: Jeddah, Saudi Arabia.
57 member states (Muslim-majority countries).

Section 15

BPSC Trap Patterns — World Geography

15 most common traps even prepared candidates fall for
⚠ TRAP 1 — Capital City Confusion

Australia → Canberra, NOT Sydney. USA → Washington D.C., NOT New York. Brazil → Brasília, NOT Rio. Turkey → Ankara, NOT Istanbul. Canada → Ottawa, NOT Toronto. Famous city ≠ capital.

⚠ TRAP 2 — Caspian "Sea" is a Lake

Caspian "Sea" is the world's largest lake, NOT a sea (despite name). Same for Dead Sea (lake) and Aral Sea (lake). BPSC tests this distinction repeatedly.

⚠ TRAP 3 — Pampas is NOT Mediterranean

Pampas (Argentina) is a temperate grassland (steppe-type), NOT Mediterranean climate. Mediterranean climate is found around the Mediterranean Sea, California, Cape Town, Perth, Central Chile.

⚠ TRAP 4 — Spring Tides Aren't About Spring Season

Spring tides occur on full moon and new moon — NOT in the spring season. Name comes from "to spring up" (tide rises high). Trick question.

⚠ TRAP 5 — Earth Closer to Sun in January, Not July

Earth is at perihelion (closest to Sun) in January; aphelion (farthest) in July. Seasons caused by axial tilt, NOT distance. Counterintuitive but tested.

⚠ TRAP 6 — Largest Desert is Antarctic, Not Sahara

If asked "largest desert (any)" → Antarctica. If asked "largest hot desert" → Sahara. Read the question carefully.

⚠ TRAP 7 — Continent in All 4 Hemispheres

Africa is the only continent that lies in all 4 hemispheres. Don't choose Asia (which doesn't cross Equator significantly).

⚠ TRAP 8 — Mt. Everest Height Updated

Mt. Everest's official height is 8,849 m (updated by Nepal-China survey in 2020). Earlier figure 8,848 m. Use the latest 8,849 m.

⚠ TRAP 9 — Highest Peak in India

Kangchenjunga (8,586 m) is highest peak in India — but it's on India-Nepal border. Nanda Devi (7,816 m) is highest peak entirely within India (Uttarakhand).

⚠ TRAP 10 — Tropic of Cancer in India

Passes through 8 states: Gujarat, Rajasthan, Madhya Pradesh, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand, West Bengal, Tripura, Mizoram. NOT through UP, Bihar (mostly), Maharashtra, Punjab, Haryana — common confusion.

⚠ TRAP 11 — UN ≠ All Specialized Agencies

WTO and OPEC are NOT UN specialized agencies — they are independent international organizations. WHO, UNESCO, FAO, IMF, World Bank ARE UN agencies.

⚠ TRAP 12 — Equator Doesn't Cross Latin America in 3 Places

The Equator passes through 13 countries — but in S. America, only through Ecuador, Colombia, and Brazil. NOT through Peru, Bolivia, or Venezuela.

⚠ TRAP 13 — Pluto Is Not the 9th Planet

Solar system has 8 planets, not 9. Pluto reclassified as a "dwarf planet" by IAU in 2006.

⚠ TRAP 14 — South Sudan is the Newest Country

South Sudan (2011) is the world's newest UN member state — split from Sudan. Don't assume "Eswatini" or "Timor-Leste" are newest (they're earlier).

⚠ TRAP 15 — UN Has 193 Members, Not 195

UN has 193 member states. Vatican City and Palestine are observer states, NOT members. Don't say 195.
