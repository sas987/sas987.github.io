★ How BPSC Tests World Geography (Last 10 Years)

Analysis of 63rd–70th BPSC papers reveals BPSC's world-geography pattern is different from UPSC. BPSC asks:

  • Direct factual questions: "Which is the largest desert?", "Tropic of Cancer passes through which countries?", "Largest ocean?"
  • Map-location questions: "Strait of Hormuz separates which two regions?", "Suez Canal connects which two seas?"
  • NCERT-aligned content: Class 6–11 NCERT level — NOT UPSC-level analytical depth
  • Country-fact questions: Capitals, currencies, recent name changes (Burma → Myanmar, Ceylon → Sri Lanka)
  • NO trick questions or multi-statement format typical of UPSC — BPSC is straightforward

"BPSC's world geography is simpler than UPSC's — but you must cover all factual ground completely. Skipping a single continent or ocean = 1–2 marks lost. Master every fact in this guide and no question can surprise you."

Foundational planetary facts — 1 question per paper guaranteed

TypeTerrestrial / Inner / Rocky planet (with Mercury, Venus, Mars)
Equatorial diameter~12,756 km
Polar diameter~12,714 km (Earth is slightly oblate spheroid — flattened at poles)
Equatorial circumference~40,075 km
Surface area~510 million sq km (70.8% water, 29.2% land)
Distance from Sun~149.6 million km (1 Astronomical Unit, AU)
Sun's light reaches Earth in~8 minutes 20 seconds
Only natural satelliteMoon — distance ~3,84,400 km; Moon's light reaches Earth in ~1.3 seconds
Earth's nicknames"Blue Planet" (water), "Goldilocks Planet" (just-right zone for life)
Age of Earth~4.54 billion years
Solar System bodies1 Sun + 8 planets + 5 dwarf planets + 200+ moons + asteroids/comets
8 planets in orderMercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune ("My Very Educated Mother Just Served Us Noodles")
Planet-Specific Facts BPSC Tests
  • Mercury — closest to Sun; smallest planet; no atmosphere; no satellites
  • Venus — hottest planet; "Earth's twin" (similar size); rotates clockwise (retrograde); "Morning/Evening Star"
  • Mars — "Red Planet" (iron oxide); 2 moons (Phobos, Deimos); Mt. Olympus = solar system's tallest mountain
  • Jupiter — largest planet; "Failed Star"; Great Red Spot (giant storm); 95+ moons; Ganymede largest moon
  • Saturn — most prominent rings; least dense (would float in water); Titan (largest moon) has thick atmosphere
  • Uranus — rotates on its side (98° tilt); discovered 1781
  • Neptune — farthest from Sun; coldest planet's atmosphere; discovered 1846
  • Pluto — reclassified as dwarf planet in 2006 by IAU; now part of Kuiper Belt
PYQ Pattern — BPSC
"Which planet is known as the 'morning star' as well as the 'evening star'?"
Venus — visible just before sunrise (morning star) or just after sunset (evening star)
PYQ Pattern — BPSC
"How many planets does our solar system have, after Pluto's reclassification?"
8 planets — Pluto reclassified as dwarf planet by IAU in 2006

Earth's Motions, Seasons & Time Concepts

Rotation, revolution, equinoxes, solstices, time zones — high-frequency in BPSC
Two Motions of Earth
MotionDescriptionDurationEffect
RotationEarth spins on its axis (West to East)23 hr 56 min 4 sec (sidereal day) — "1 day"Day & night, deflection of winds (Coriolis), tides
RevolutionEarth orbits the Sun (elliptical path)365 days 5 hr 48 min 46 sec = "1 year"Seasons, leap year (every 4 years to adjust extra ~6 hours)
Equinoxes & Solstices
EventDateSun is overhead atSignificance
Vernal (Spring) EquinoxMarch 21EquatorDay = Night across world; spring in N. Hemisphere
Summer SolsticeJune 21Tropic of Cancer (23.5° N)Longest day in N. Hemisphere; shortest in S. Hemisphere
Autumnal EquinoxSeptember 23EquatorDay = Night again; autumn in N. Hemisphere
Winter SolsticeDecember 22Tropic of Capricorn (23.5° S)Shortest day in N. Hemisphere; longest in S. Hemisphere
Memorise dates: 21–23–21–22 → Mar 21, Sept 23 = equinoxes (equal day-night); Jun 21, Dec 22 = solstices (extreme day-night). At equinoxes, Sun is overhead at Equator. At June solstice, overhead at Tropic of Cancer. At December solstice, overhead at Tropic of Capricorn.
Aphelion & Perihelion
AphelionEarth farthest from Sun (~152 million km) — around July 4
PerihelionEarth nearest to Sun (~147 million km) — around January 3
⚠ Common Trap

Earth is nearest to Sun in January (during N. Hemisphere winter), and farthest in July (during N. Hemisphere summer). Seasons are caused by Earth's axial tilt (23.5°), NOT by distance from Sun. Frequently asked as a misconception trap.

Time Zones & International Date Line
Total time zones24 — Earth divided every 15° longitude (360° ÷ 24)
Time per longitude4 minutes per 1° of longitude (15° × 4 min = 60 min)
Prime Meridian (0°)Greenwich, London — passes through Royal Observatory; reference for GMT/UTC
International Date Line (IDL)180° meridian — runs through Pacific Ocean; bends to avoid land (Russia, Aleutian Islands, Fiji, Kiribati)
Crossing IDL westward (Asia → America)Gain 1 day (date moves backward)
Crossing IDL eastward (America → Asia)Lose 1 day (date moves forward)
India's Standard Time (IST)UTC + 5:30 hours; based on 82.5° E longitude (passing through Mirzapur, UP)

Section 04

Earth's Interior Structure

Crust, mantle, core — basic earth science from NCERT; 1 question per paper
LayerDepthComposition / StateDensity
Crust0–35 km (continental); 5–10 km (oceanic)Solid; Continental crust = SiAl (Silica + Aluminium); Oceanic crust = SiMa (Silica + Magnesium)2.7–3.0 g/cm³
Mantle35 km – 2,900 kmSolid (upper) → Plastic (asthenosphere, ~100–200 km) → Solid (lower); composed of silicates rich in iron & magnesium3.4–5.6 g/cm³
Outer Core2,900 – 5,150 kmLiquid; mostly Iron + Nickel (Nife); generates Earth's magnetic field9.9–12.2 g/cm³
Inner Core5,150 – 6,371 km (center)Solid due to immense pressure; Iron + Nickel; temperature ~5,500°C12.6–13.0 g/cm³
Key Discontinuities (Boundaries)
  • Mohorovičić Discontinuity (Moho) — between Crust and Mantle
  • Gutenberg Discontinuity — between Mantle and Outer Core (~2,900 km)
  • Lehmann Discontinuity — between Outer Core and Inner Core (~5,150 km)
  • Conrad Discontinuity — within continental crust (between SiAl and SiMa layers)
  • Repetti Discontinuity — between upper and lower mantle
Plate Tectonics — Foundation

Earth's lithosphere (crust + uppermost mantle) is broken into ~7 major plates and several minor plates that move slowly over the asthenosphere.

7 Major Tectonic Plates
  • Pacific Plate (largest)
  • African Plate
  • Eurasian Plate
  • North American Plate
  • South American Plate
  • Indo-Australian Plate
  • Antarctic Plate
Plate Boundary Types
  • Convergent: plates collide → Himalayas, Andes, ocean trenches
  • Divergent: plates separate → Mid-Atlantic Ridge, East African Rift
  • Transform: plates slide past → San Andreas Fault (California)
PYQ Pattern — BPSC
"The boundary between the Earth's crust and mantle is known as?"
Mohorovičić Discontinuity (Moho)

Section 09

The 5 Oceans of the World

Sizes, depths, and bordering continents — guaranteed 1–2 questions per paper
Pacific Ocean ★ #1 LARGEST · DEEPEST
Area: ~165 million sq km (largest — covers ~30% of Earth's surface; almost as big as all other oceans combined).
Average depth: ~4,280 m. Deepest point: Challenger Deep in the Mariana Trench (~10,994 m — deepest point on Earth).
Bordering continents: North America, South America (east); Asia, Australia (west); Antarctica (south).
Major features: "Ring of Fire" (Pacific volcanic belt — 75% of world's volcanoes & 90% earthquakes); islands of Indonesia, Philippines, Japan; Hawaiian Islands.
Named by: Ferdinand Magellan (1520) — "Mar Pacifico" (peaceful sea).
Atlantic Ocean #2 LARGEST
Area: ~106 million sq km (~20% of Earth's surface).
Average depth: ~3,646 m. Deepest point: Puerto Rico Trench (~8,605 m — Milwaukee Deep).
Bordering continents: N. America, S. America (west); Europe, Africa (east); Antarctica (south).
Major features: Mid-Atlantic Ridge (longest underwater mountain range, divergent plate boundary); Sargasso Sea (only sea without coastline — "sea within an ocean"); Bermuda Triangle.
Shape: "S-shaped"; separates "Old World" (Europe-Africa-Asia) from "New World" (Americas).
Iceland sits on the Mid-Atlantic Ridge (volcanic island).
Indian Ocean #3 LARGEST
Area: ~70.5 million sq km (~14% of Earth's surface).
Average depth: ~3,741 m. Deepest point: Java Trench / Sunda Trench (~7,725 m, off Indonesia).
Bordering continents: Africa (west); Asia (north); Australia (east); Antarctica (south).
Major features: Only ocean named after a country (India); monsoon system (seasonal wind reversal); chokepoints — Strait of Hormuz, Strait of Malacca, Bab-el-Mandeb.
Maritime trade: ~80% of world's seaborne oil trade passes through Indian Ocean.
Major islands: Madagascar (4th largest island in world), Sri Lanka, Maldives, Seychelles, Mauritius, Indonesia.
2004 Indian Ocean Tsunami: ~230,000 deaths — caused by 9.1 magnitude earthquake near Sumatra.
Southern Ocean (Antarctic Ocean) #4 · NEWEST RECOGNITION
Area: ~21.96 million sq km. Surrounds Antarctica.
Boundary: 60° South latitude.
Recognition: Officially recognized by IHO (International Hydrographic Organization) in 2000; National Geographic recognized it in June 2021 (frequently asked!).
Special feature: Antarctic Circumpolar Current — the only ocean current that flows around the entire globe uninterrupted.
Climate impact: Influences global weather patterns; massive heat sink.
Iceberg source: Most of world's icebergs originate from Antarctic ice shelves here.
Arctic Ocean #5 SMALLEST · SHALLOWEST
Area: ~14.06 million sq km (smallest ocean).
Average depth: ~1,038 m (shallowest of 5 oceans).
Bordering continents: N. America, Asia, Europe (Greenland, Russia, Canada coastlines).
Frozen surface: Mostly covered by sea ice year-round; ice extent decreasing due to climate change.
North Pole located here — beneath the ice, in international waters.
Major features: Lomonosov Ridge (underwater mountain range); seasonal Northern Sea Route opening as ice melts.
Bering Strait (Russia-Alaska) connects Arctic with Pacific Ocean.
★ Quick Ocean Records (Memorise All)
  • Largest ocean: Pacific (~165 million sq km)
  • Smallest ocean: Arctic (~14 million sq km)
  • Deepest ocean: Pacific (Mariana Trench, ~10,994 m)
  • Shallowest ocean: Arctic (~1,038 m avg)
  • Most saline ocean: Atlantic (avg ~37 ppt)
  • Least saline ocean: Arctic (~28-32 ppt — freshwater inflow)
  • Newest ocean (recognized): Southern Ocean (2000)
  • Only ocean named after a country: Indian Ocean
  • Ocean with no coastline: N/A — but Sargasso Sea (in Atlantic) has no coastline
