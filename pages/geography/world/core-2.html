Atmosphere — Layers & Composition

Most fundamental atmospheric structure — appears in mixed Geography-Science questions
LayerAltitudeKey Features
Troposphere0 – 12 km (avg)All weather phenomena occur here; ~75% atmospheric mass; temperature decreases with height (~6.5°C/km)
Stratosphere12 – 50 kmContains the Ozone Layer (~25 km); temperature increases with height; jet aircraft fly here for stable air
Mesosphere50 – 80 kmColdest layer (-90°C); meteors burn up here
Thermosphere (Ionosphere)80 – 700 kmContains ionized gases; auroras (Northern/Southern Lights); reflects radio waves; ISS orbits here
Exosphere700 – 10,000 kmOutermost layer; merges with space; satellites orbit here
Troposphere is BPSC's most-asked atmospheric layer — questions like "In which layer do all weather phenomena occur?" or "Which layer's name comes from Greek 'tropos' meaning change?" The answer is always Troposphere. Also asked: Stratosphere contains the ozone layer — protecting Earth from UV radiation.

Section 02

Pressure & Wind Belts of the World

Permanent global wind belts — directly tested factually in BPSC

Earth has 7 pressure belts (4 high pressure, 3 low pressure) and corresponding wind belts. The pattern alternates by latitude.

The 7 Pressure Belts
BeltLatitudePressure TypeNotes
Equatorial Low (Doldrums)0° – 5°LowCalm, wet, rising air; "Doldrums" — sailing ships got stuck for days
Subtropical High (Horse Latitudes)~30° N & SHighSinking air, dry; most world deserts here (Sahara, Thar, Kalahari)
Subpolar Low~60° N & SLowPolar fronts; cyclones form here
Polar High~90° N & SHighCold dense air; descending
The 3 Major Wind Belts (Permanent Winds)
Trade Winds EQUATORIAL From subtropical highs → equator
Direction: NE Trade Winds (N. Hemisphere); SE Trade Winds (S. Hemisphere) — both blow toward equator.
Why "Trade": Used by sailing ships for centuries to cross oceans; reliable direction.
Coriolis effect: Why they curve — Earth's rotation deflects them right (N) or left (S).
Brings: Heavy rainfall on east coasts of continents (windward side); deserts form on western interiors (leeward).
Westerlies MID-LATITUDE From subtropical highs → subpolar lows (~30°-60°)
Direction: SW (N. Hemisphere); NW (S. Hemisphere) — blow eastward, hence "Westerlies" (winds coming FROM west).
"Roaring Forties": Strongest westerlies between 40°-50° S — used by clipper ships to sail Australia/NZ to S. America.
"Furious Fifties" & "Shrieking Sixties": Even stronger southern hemisphere westerlies at 50° & 60° S.
Brings: Bring rain to W. coasts of continents (e.g., NW Europe, NW USA, Chile, NZ).
Polar Easterlies POLAR From polar highs → subpolar lows (~60°-90°)
Direction: NE (Arctic); SE (Antarctic).
Character: Cold, dry, weak winds.
Polar Front: Where cold polar easterlies meet warm westerlies — forms cyclones (mid-latitude depressions).
Westerlies are highly tested in BPSC — questions on their direction, "Roaring Forties", and their role in bringing rain to west coasts of continents. Also asked: "Trade winds blow toward which direction?" Answer: toward the Equator from subtropical highs (NOT away from equator).
Periodic Winds & Jet Streams
Wind TypeDescription
Monsoon WindsSeasonal reversal of winds; SW monsoon (June–Sept) brings rain to India; NE monsoon (Oct–Dec) brings rain to TN coast
Land & Sea BreezeDaily — sea breeze (sea→land in day); land breeze (land→sea at night)
Mountain & Valley BreezeUp-valley wind in day; down-valley at night
Jet StreamsHigh-altitude (10-15 km), high-speed (200-400 km/h) westerly winds in upper troposphere; Subtropical Jet Stream brings western disturbances to N. India in winter
The Subtropical Westerly Jet Stream brings western disturbances from Mediterranean → bringing winter rain to North India (and N. Bihar). Frequently asked in BPSC mixed with India climate questions.

5 major climate groups, defined by temperature + precipitation — high BPSC frequency for "type of climate found in..."

Important Climate Sub-Types — BPSC Tests These Specifically
Equatorial / Tropical Rainforest (Af) TROPICAL
Location: Within 5°-10° of equator — Amazon Basin, Congo Basin, Indonesia, parts of West Africa.
Climate: Hot (~27°C avg) and wet all year (>200 cm rainfall); no dry season; humid.
Vegetation: Selvas (Amazon name); dense tropical rainforests; tall trees, lianas, epiphytes; multilayered canopy.
Animals: Jaguars, monkeys, parrots, tropical reptiles.
Tropical Savanna (Aw) TROPICAL
Location: Between 5°-20° latitude — East Africa (Kenya, Tanzania), parts of S. America (Llanos), N. Australia.
Climate: Wet summer + dry winter; temperatures 20-30°C.
Vegetation: Tall grasses + scattered trees (acacia, baobab).
Animals: Africa's "Big Five" — Lion, Leopard, Elephant, Rhino, Buffalo; Zebras, Giraffes, Wildebeest.
Mediterranean (Cs) TEMPERATE
Location: 30°-45° latitude on WEST coasts of continents — countries around Mediterranean Sea (Italy, Spain, Greece, Turkey), California (USA), Central Chile, SW Australia (Perth), Cape Town (S. Africa).
Climate: Wet mild winters + hot dry summers (unique combo!).
Vegetation: Olive, Citrus (Orange, Lemon), Grapes (wine), Cork Oak, Fig — drought-resistant evergreens; "Chaparral" in California.
Why western coasts? Westerlies bring rain in winter; subtropical highs bring dry summer.
Steppe / Mid-Latitude Grassland (BSk) SEMI-ARID
Location: Continental interiors — Russia/Ukraine (Steppes), N. America (Prairies), Argentina (Pampas), S. Africa (Veld), Australia (Downs).
Climate: Hot summers, cold winters; moderate rainfall (25-75 cm).
Vegetation: Short grasses; treeless plains; "Wheat baskets of the world".
Tundra (ET) POLAR
Location: 60°-75° latitude — Arctic regions of Canada, Alaska, Russia, Greenland.
Climate: Cold all year; brief summer (~10°C); long dark winters; precipitation low (snow).
Vegetation: No trees; mosses, lichens, dwarf shrubs; permafrost in subsoil.
Animals: Polar bears, reindeer/caribou, arctic foxes.
Taiga / Boreal Forest (Df, Dw) CONTINENTAL
Location: 50°-70° latitude — Russia (Siberia), Canada, Scandinavia. Largest forest belt in world.
Climate: Long cold winters, short cool summers.
Vegetation: Coniferous evergreens — Pine, Spruce, Fir, Larch.
Importance: Major timber source.
Mediterranean climate question — "In which area is Mediterranean climate found?" Possible options: California, Cape Town, Pampas (TRAP — Pampas is steppe, NOT Mediterranean), Perth. Answer: California (or any Mediterranean-coastal-temperate option). Pampas is the trap — it's a steppe/grassland. This exact trap appeared in past BPSC papers.
Tropical Savanna grasslands have appeared in BPSC — "Which is a tropical grassland?" Answer is Savanna. Don't confuse with Steppe (mid-latitude), Pampas (Argentine), or Prairie (N. American) which are all temperate grasslands.

Section 06

Major Ocean Currents — Warm Currents

★ Highest BPSC frequency — match current to ocean/effect

"Ocean currents are nature's heat conveyors. Warm currents bring rainfall and moderate climate; cold currents bring deserts on adjacent coasts. Master these 8 currents and you've covered every ocean-current question in BPSC."

Gulf Stream ★ MOST FAMOUS North Atlantic · Warm
Origin: Gulf of Mexico → flows along east coast of USA → crosses Atlantic to Europe.
Effect: Makes Western Europe (UK, Ireland, Norway) much warmer than its latitude would suggest. London is at same latitude as Hudson Bay (Canada) — but London is mild, Hudson Bay is frozen.
Combined with: North Atlantic Drift (extension into Europe).
BPSC favorite: "Why is Western Europe warm despite high latitude?" — Gulf Stream + N. Atlantic Drift.
North Atlantic Drift WARM Atlantic · Continuation of Gulf Stream
Extension of Gulf Stream that flows past UK and Norway, keeping their ports ice-free year-round (e.g., Norwegian port of Hammerfest — northernmost ice-free port).
Effect: NW Europe enjoys mild winters; westerlies carry warm moist air inland → high rainfall in W. coast Europe.
Kuroshio Current "BLACK CURRENT" North Pacific · Warm
Origin: Equatorial Pacific → flows past Philippines, Taiwan, Japan.
Name: "Kuroshio" = "Black Current" in Japanese (deep blue color, dark from above).
Effect: Pacific equivalent of Gulf Stream; warms east coast of Japan; combines with North Pacific Drift to warm NW USA/British Columbia.
Brazil Current WARM South Atlantic
Flows along east coast of South America (Brazil) southward; warm; supports tropical fishery.
Agulhas Current WARM SW Indian Ocean · Off SE Africa
Flows along east coast of South Africa southward; one of fastest ocean currents.
East Australian Current WARM SW Pacific
Flows along east coast of Australia southward; brings rain to Queensland; supports Great Barrier Reef.
Norwegian Current WARM · ARCTIC Continuation of N. Atlantic Drift
Flows along Norwegian coast into Arctic; keeps northern ports of Norway ice-free.
Gulf Stream's effect on Europe is BPSC's #1 ocean current question. Pattern: "Why is the climate of Western Europe milder than its latitude?" → Gulf Stream / North Atlantic Drift. Also asked: "Kuroshio is also called?" → Black Current.
Section 07

Major Ocean Currents — Cold Currents

Cold currents flow toward equator from polar regions — they create coastal deserts
Humboldt Current (Peru Current) ★ MOST TESTED South Pacific · Cold
Origin: Antarctica → flows north along west coast of S. America (Chile, Peru).
Effect: Creates the Atacama Desert — world's driest desert (cold current cools air → no rain). Cold current brings nutrient-rich water → world's richest fishery (anchovies).
El Niño disruption: When Humboldt weakens, El Niño event occurs (warm water replaces cold) → fishery collapses → global climate impacts.
California Current COLD North Pacific · Off W. USA
Flows south along California coast; cools coastal area; creates fog in San Francisco (warm air over cold water = condensation); supports rich fisheries.
Canary Current COLD Off NW Africa (Atlantic)
Flows south along NW African coast (Morocco, Western Sahara, Mauritania); contributes to Sahara Desert's coastal aridity; rich in fish.
Benguela Current COLD Off SW Africa (Atlantic)
Flows north along W. coast of S. Africa (Namibia, Angola); creates Namib Desert (a coastal cold-current desert).
Labrador Current COLD NW Atlantic · Off NE Canada
Flows south from Arctic past Newfoundland (Canada). Where it meets warm Gulf Stream → creates Grand Banks fishery (one of world's richest fishing grounds, off Newfoundland) AND dense fog.
1912 Titanic incident: Iceberg from Labrador Current sank Titanic.
Oyashio Current COLD NW Pacific · Off NE Japan
"Parent Current" — flows south from Bering Sea past Japan. Meets warm Kuroshio → great fishing zone in NE Japan; nutrient-rich.
West Australian Current COLD SE Indian Ocean · Off W. Australia
Flows north along W. coast of Australia; creates aridity (Great Sandy / Gibson Deserts on adjacent coast).
Antarctic Circumpolar Current COLD · UNIQUE Circles Antarctica
The only current that flows uninterrupted around the entire globe; in Southern Ocean. Drives global thermohaline circulation.
"Coastal deserts are formed by cold ocean currents" — important BPSC concept. Examples: Atacama-Humboldt, Namib-Benguela, Sahara coast-Canary. Also asked: "Where do warm and cold currents meet to form rich fisheries?" Answer: Newfoundland (Labrador-Gulf Stream), Japan (Kuroshio-Oyashio).
★ The Big 4 Cold Currents = 4 Coastal Deserts
  • Humboldt Current → Atacama Desert (Chile/Peru)
  • Benguela Current → Namib Desert (Namibia/Angola)
  • Canary Current → Sahara coast (NW Africa)
  • West Australian Current → Great Sandy / Gibson Deserts
PYQ Pattern — BPSC
"The Atacama Desert in Chile is caused by which ocean current?"
Humboldt Current (Peru Current) — cold, prevents rainfall along Pacific coast
Section 08

El Niño, La Niña, ENSO

Frequently in news + tested factually — affects Indian monsoon
El Niño "THE LITTLE BOY" Warm phase of ENSO
What happens: Trade winds weaken → warm water from W. Pacific moves to E. Pacific (Peru coast) → Humboldt Current weakens.
Effects:
  • • Weak Indian monsoon → drought in India
  • • Heavy rainfall and floods in S. America (Peru, Ecuador)
  • • Drought in Australia, Indonesia
  • • Warmer global temperatures
Frequency: Every 2-7 years; named by Peruvian fishermen ("Christ Child" — appears around Christmas).
La Niña "THE LITTLE GIRL" Cool phase of ENSO
What happens: Trade winds strengthen → cold water remains in E. Pacific → opposite of El Niño.
Effects:
  • • Strong Indian monsoon → good rainfall in India
  • • Drought in S. America (Peru, Ecuador)
  • • Heavy rainfall and floods in Australia, Indonesia
  • • Cooler global temperatures
"Effect of El Niño on Indian monsoon" — confirmed BPSC question pattern. Answer: weakens monsoon, causes drought in India. La Niña = opposite (good monsoon).
Other Climate Phenomena
ENSOEl Niño Southern Oscillation — broad term covering both El Niño + La Niña + neutral phases
Indian Ocean Dipole (IOD)Similar to ENSO but in Indian Ocean; Positive IOD = good monsoon for India; Negative IOD = poor monsoon
Walker CirculationThe atmospheric circulation pattern across Pacific; reverses during El Niño
Madden-Julian Oscillation (MJO)Eastward-moving rainfall pattern in tropical atmosphere; affects monsoon onset

Volcanoes & the Ring of Fire

Geographic and current-affairs flavored questions in BPSC
Pacific Ring of Fire ★ MOST TESTED "Circum-Pacific Belt"
Length: ~40,000 km horseshoe-shaped belt around the Pacific Ocean.
Volcanoes: ~75% of world's active & dormant volcanoes (~750-915).
Earthquakes: ~90% of world's earthquakes occur here.
Why? Result of plate tectonics — multiple plate boundaries (Pacific Plate meeting other plates).
Countries on Ring of Fire: Chile, Peru, Mexico, USA (West Coast), Canada, Russia (Kamchatka), Japan, Philippines, Indonesia, Papua New Guinea, New Zealand.
"About 75% of world's volcanoes are located in?" → Pacific Ring of Fire. Also: "Which country has the most active volcanoes?" → Indonesia (~75 active) — sits on Ring of Fire between multiple plates.

Section 15

Important Canals of the World

★ Suez and Panama are most-tested in BPSC
Suez Canal ★ MOST IMPORTANT Egypt · Mediterranean ↔ Red Sea
Length: 193 km. Connects: Mediterranean Sea ↔ Red Sea (via Gulf of Suez).
Country: Egypt; passes through Isthmus of Suez.
Built: 1869 by Ferdinand de Lesseps (French engineer).
Significance: Connects Europe to Asia without going around Africa (Cape of Good Hope); saves ~7,000 km on Asia-Europe shipping.
Special: NOT a lock canal (water levels are equal). Single waterway, no locks.
2021 blockage: Container ship Ever Given blocked Suez for 6 days; disrupted global trade.
Nationalization: 1956 by Egypt's President Nasser → Suez Crisis (war with UK, France, Israel).
Panama Canal ★ 2ND MOST IMPORTANT Panama · Atlantic ↔ Pacific
Length: 82 km. Connects: Atlantic Ocean (Caribbean Sea) ↔ Pacific Ocean.
Country: Panama; cuts across the Isthmus of Panama (narrowest point of Americas).
Built: 1914 (USA, after French failure); transferred to Panama in 1999.
Special: Lock canal — uses Gatun Lake (artificial); ships raised 26 m up and down through 3 lock systems.
Significance: Saves ~13,000 km vs going around Cape Horn (S. America's tip). Vital for US East-West coast shipping.
Kiel Canal EUROPE Germany · North Sea ↔ Baltic Sea
Length: 98 km. Built 1895. Most-used artificial waterway in the world (by ship count). Allows ships to bypass long route around Denmark.
Grand Canal (Beijing-Hangzhou) CHINA · ANCIENT World's longest artificial canal
Length: 1,776 km — longest canal in the world. Built: 5th century BC (oldest existing canal); expanded in Sui Dynasty (7th century AD). Connects Yellow River and Yangtze. UNESCO World Heritage.
Erie Canal USA Hudson River ↔ Lake Erie
584 km in New York state. Opened 1825. Historic; once economically transformative for NY-Midwest trade.
Volga-Don Canal RUSSIA Volga River ↔ Don River
101 km. Connects Caspian Sea to Black Sea via rivers. Built 1952.
Suez Canal connects Mediterranean Sea and Red Sea — confirmed BPSC question. Also asked: "Panama Canal is a lock canal" (TRUE — Suez is NOT a lock canal). "Which is the longest canal in the world?" → Grand Canal of China.
★ Canal vs Strait — Common Confusion
  • Strait: Natural narrow water body connecting two larger seas/oceans (e.g., Hormuz, Malacca)
  • Canal: Artificial (man-made) waterway (e.g., Suez, Panama, Kiel)
  • BPSC sometimes mixes these in match-the-pair questions — be careful
PYQ Pattern — BPSC
"The Suez Canal connects which two seas?"
Mediterranean Sea and Red Sea — across Isthmus of Suez, Egypt
PYQ Pattern — BPSC
"The Panama Canal connects which two oceans?"
Atlantic Ocean and Pacific Ocean — across Isthmus of Panama (Central America)
